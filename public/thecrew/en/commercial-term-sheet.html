05 · What We Need From You

For the pilot

One client brief, brand guidelines, access to the client's historical campaign data (performance metrics, past creative, audience segments), and one point of contact for the two review sessions.

For ongoing

Connector access (Notion workspace, Slack channel, Google Calendar), client brand files per onboarding, and commitment to the review cadence — the system pauses for human approval, so response time affects throughput.

07 · Three Ways to Work Together
Model A

Managed Service

K&B runs the Command Center on your behalf. You send briefs, we run the system, you review at checkpoints. Lowest effort for you. Highest per-client fee.

Best for: agencies testing the model with 1–3 clients before committing team resources.

Model B

Embedded Partnership

Your team learns to operate the system. K&B handles setup, training, and ongoing support. You run day-to-day. Lower per-client cost. Your team builds the capability.

Best for: agencies scaling to 5–15 clients who want the competency in-house.

Model C

White-Label

The Command Center runs under your brand. Your clients see your name, your portal, your workflow. K&B provides the infrastructure and ongoing development. Revenue share model.

Best for: agencies building AI into their core proposition at 15+ clients.

Every engagement starts with the pilot. Model A, B, or C is a conversation we have after you've seen the system work on a real brief. The pilot is designed to give you the evidence to make that decision.
