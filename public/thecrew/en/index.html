Your decisions take months — and you only test them in the market

A new product launch. A GTM strategy for a new segment. A repositioning after an acquisition. The analysis takes weeks. The stakeholder alignment takes months. And only when you go live do you find out whether your assumptions were right.

Safe — and trained on your data Standalone

The Crew doesn't run on a shared instance. You get your own ring-fenced version — trained on your brand, your market and your own data, and that's where it stays.

🔒
Your own environment
Runs in your cloud or tenant. Never shared with other customers. Full control over access and logging.
🔐

No foundation-model training on your content. No commingling with other accounts. Removable on request.

What this frees up: Your team focuses on strategy, client relationships, and creative decision-making. Not data entry and copy formatting.

4-Week Proof of Concept

Week 1

Onboarding & Context

Load brand guidelines, CRM data, analytics export, competitor list. The Lookout & Spyglass run baseline analysis. Outputs: baseline performance map & competitive positioning gap analysis.

Week 2

Strategy Sprint

Navigator synthesizes intel into positioning + channel mix + creative direction. Output: falsifiable strategy document + 3 content calendar templates (each channel). Human review & refinement.

Week 3

First Execution

Cartographer maps funnel. Scribe + Quartermaster produce first campaign (ad copy, email sequence, media plan). All pieces QA'd. Output: live campaign + performance baseline.

Week 4

Proof & Handoff

Chronometer & Helmsman analyze week 1 results. Ship's Log delivers first performance narrative. Output: proof of ROI + scaling roadmap + agent training summary for your team.

What Stays Human

Decision Type Why Who Decides
Strategic positioning Requires context, brand history, market intuition — AI can synthesize, but humans must approve the "story" Chief Strategist / C-level
Budget allocation Business priorities & constraints (cash flow, organizational goals) — beyond data Finance / VP Marketing
Creative direction Brand voice, tone shifts, cultural sensitivity — AI can draft, but humans own brand expression Creative Director / Content Lead
Experiment design Statistical rigor & business sense together — what to test & how to interpret Growth Lead + Data Analyst
Client communication Relationships, trust, nuance — AI supports narrative, humans deliver Account Manager / Director
Crisis response Real-time judgment, stakeholder alignment — AI can flag, humans decide action Leadership / Client Management
